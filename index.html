World Population Exploratory Data Analysis

I performed an exploratory data analysis on world population data spanning from 1970 to 2022 using Python to uncover trends in population growth, density, and regional distribution. Asia remained the most populated continent as of 2022, with 96 million people, accounting for a significant portion of the global population, with China standing out as the most populated country, contributing approximately 17% of the total world population. Russia happened to be the largest country by land area, covering about 17 million square kilometers, while Africa recorded the higest population growth rate, growing at an average of 1.02% every 10 years over the analyzed period.

Motor Vehicle Theft Analysis

I analyzed motor vehicle theft data in New Zealand from october 2021 to April 2022 and identified key trends across regions, car makes, colors, and model years. Using Excel, I visualized the insights in an interactive dashboard to highlight high-risk areas and theft patterns. A total of 4,509 vehicles were reported stolen during the 7-month period, with Auckland being the most vulnerable region, accounting for 34% of all reported thefts. Toyota was the most fequently stolen brand making up 16% of the total stolen vehicles, while the highest number of thefts occurred in March 2022, with 23.2% of total cased reported in that month alone.
